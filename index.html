How PathForge works

Not a lesson.
An experience.

Every PathForge story is two things in sequence — a video and a simulation. Together they take about 20 minutes and leave a young person thinking about decisions in a way a classroom rarely does.

1
The video · 4–5 minutes

An inventor's story — told as a decision

Every PathForge story opens with a short animated video about a real person who changed the world. But we do not tell it as history. We tell it as the moment before the decision — the fork in the road where everything could have gone differently. Young people see that breakthroughs happen because of how someone chose to think, not because they were born special.

Example — The Boy Who Read With His Fingers
Louis Braille, Paris, 1824. He is 15 years old and blind. He has just heard about a military code soldiers use to read messages in the dark — without light, without sound. Nobody has thought to use it for reading books. Louis looks at the dots and dashes and asks one question nobody else thought to ask. What if this could work for us?
2
The simulation · 15 minutes

You are in the decision. What do you choose?

The video freezes at the critical moment — and the young person takes over. They face the same decision the inventor faced, with four realistic choices. Each decision leads to a consequence. Each consequence leads to a second, harder decision. There is no single right answer — only different paths, each with something to teach.

Inside the simulation
You are Louis. The military code is in your hands. Your teacher says it is too complicated for blind students. Your classmates are not sure it will work. You have one evening before the school decision is made. A — Test it on yourself tonight. B — Ask your teacher to give you more time. C — Share the idea with your closest friend first. D — Present it to the school headmaster directly. Every choice leads somewhere different.
3
The debrief · 5 minutes

Reflect. Name what you learned. Make it yours.

After the simulation, three questions invite the young person to reflect on what they chose and why. A personalised summary — unique to the exact path they took through the story — names the skills and values their decisions demonstrated. Every child gets a different ending, because every child made different choices.

What the debrief asks
"Louis was 15 and had no laboratory, no funding, and no support from most adults around him. What do you think gave him the courage to keep going anyway?" No right answer. Just thinking.
4
The career reveal

Meet the real careers they just practised.

At the end of every story, PathForge shows the young person the real careers that use the skills they just demonstrated. Not a list of job titles — a specific connection between what they just did and what real professionals do every single day. This is how a 10-year-old discovers they might want to work in accessibility design, or a 13-year-old realises they have always been thinking like a social innovator.

Built for parents who want more
than another screen.

PathForge is not a game. It is not a revision app. It is a place where your child practises the skill that determines more about their future than any exam result — how to make decisions under pressure, with incomplete information, when it matters.

🎯
Career exploration from age 7
Children form career aspirations earlier than most people think. PathForge puts them inside real career decisions before limiting beliefs set in.
🧠
Values through experience
The debrief surfaces qualities like persistence, empathy, and integrity — not by lecturing, but by asking the child to name what they did and why.
📚
Aligned to the UK curriculum
Every simulation maps to PSHE outcomes and the Gatsby Benchmarks for careers education — so schools can use PathForge as a teaching tool too.

No account. No data collected.
The simulations are fully self-contained. No login, no tracking of individual children, no data stored. Safe for any age.
