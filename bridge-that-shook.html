Opening

The Bridge
That Shook

It's early morning. The construction site is quiet except for the grind of a distant forklift and the smell of damp concrete.

You're an intern — first week on the job — carrying a clipboard to the site supervisor on the other side of a half-built footbridge.

You step onto the metal deck. Halfway across—

The bridge trembles beneath your feet.

Not wind. Not a truck passing. A pulse — one second — then nothing.

You stop walking. Look down. The metal is still.

Around you, the crew keeps working. No one looked up.

You felt it. You know you did.

— What do you do? —

Scene 2

The Second Shake

That afternoon, the crew runs a test. A concrete mixer starts up on the far end of the bridge — heavy, grinding, loud.

The moment the engine hits full power—

The vibration is back. Stronger this time.

A metal beam lets out a low groan.

The supervisor is fifty metres away, back turned. The workers are focused on their tasks.

You are the closest person to the shaking point.

You have seconds.

— What do you do? —
