Building site · 6:52am

The Pour

The concrete truck is here. You just noticed the rebar is wrong.

You are a construction apprentice, six months into your training. Today the site is pouring the concrete foundation for a new block of flats — a big, expensive job that has to happen in one go. Once concrete is poured, there is no undoing it.

The concrete truck arrives at 6:52am. The pour is scheduled for 7. As you do a last check of the foundation, you notice something about the steel reinforcement bars — the "rebar" that gives concrete its strength. The spacing looks wrong to you. Wider apart than the drawings showed.

You are the most junior person on site. The rebar was signed off by an experienced worker last night. And in eight minutes, tonnes of concrete will bury it forever.

ProgressScene 1

If you are right, the foundation could be weaker than the design requires — a serious structural problem, hidden under concrete, in a building where people will live. If you are wrong, you have halted a major pour and cost the company money and time over nothing.

🧱
Why it matters
Rebar spacing is not a detail — it is what stops concrete cracking and failing under load. Get it wrong and the flaw is invisible once poured, but it can weaken the whole structure for the life of the building.

The truck is running. The site manager is watching the clock. And you have a nagging feeling you cannot ignore.
